\section{Simplification by Product Kernels}
% --- SLIDE 3: PRODUCT KERNELS (SIMPLIFICATION) ---
\begin{frame}{Practical Approach: Product Kernels}
    To simplify, we assume independence between dimensions (diagonal $\vect{H}$).
    The kernel becomes a product of univariate kernels:

    \begin{equation}
        \hat{f}(\vect{x}) = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^{n} \prod_{j=1}^{p} \frac{1}{h_j} K\left( \frac{x_j - X_{ij}}{h_j} \right)
    \end{equation}

    \begin{columns}
        \begin{column}{0.5\textwidth}
           \textbf{Advantages:}
           \begin{itemize}
               \item Reduces parameters from $p^2$ to $p$.
               \item Computationally efficient ($O(n)$).
               \item Allows different bandwidth $h_j$ for each dimension.
           \end{itemize}
        \end{column}
